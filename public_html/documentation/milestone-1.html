Personal Webpage Milestone 1:

Purpose, Audience, Goal

Purpose

I would like my PWP to be a showcase of who I am as a person by presenting the projects I have completed both in real life and digitally. My time as a nurse, teacher, woodworker, coder, and gamer has made me into a highly technical, yet personable professional. I believe that by presenting elements from my life, perspective employers will see that investing in me provides more than just my experiences in a bootcamp, but also gives them access to 15 years of problem-solving, creative solutions, and exuberant creativity, packaged in a way that is fun, engaging, and simplified for anyone to understand.

Audience

This website is designed for anyone who is looking for a competent problem solver to hire (for money!). This may include business owners looking to hire someone to build them a website, technical recruiters trying to put together a design team, or project managers who need to bring in a consultant on a project. Whomever it may be, this site assists them in filling their coding and problem-solving needs.

Goal

This project will serve several purposes: serving as a monumental showcase of my work in an interactive way; showing prospective clients, employers, and partners who I am and what I bring to the table; and as an opportunity to stretch my mind with self-imposed coding challenges. All of these however go towards my main goal and reason for taking this bootcamp in the first place, to help me find gainful employment that is: outside public education; mentally stimulating without leaving me physically and emotionally exhausted at the end of the day; and provides just enough money to give myself, my wife, and our wonderful cats a life where our basic needs are met.

Persona

Age

Profession

Bryan is a project manager for Webbo llc. a tech firm that that creates and maintains web applications. The company isn't in a great spot financially, so they've decided to take on contract work to cover costs. They just took on a contract job from hoopla.com, a much larger data processing company, to build the user interface for a survey website. Bryan has been with Webbo since they started up back in 2006. He considers himself tech-savvy with some prior coding experience but not a lot of time working as a developer.

Technology

Attitudes and behaviors

Bryan doesn't keep up with new technology as much as he would like to. He's an older, 9-5 style manager with how he runs his projects but is trying to be more flexible. Bryan likes to see initiative in his team and tends to hire "go-getters" for projects. he still has a "knows a guy who knows a guy" style of networking and has never really enjoyed digging through job sites for possible candidates.

Frustrations and needs

Bryan is looking to bring 1 or 2 jr developers onto his team to help meet the deadline that is quickly approaching. He recognizes that his team needs help keeping up with the small tasks that are taking up his higher level developers. he's willing to take a risk on new developers because his team just needs more manpower and the tasks he wants help with are relatively rote. His company is also facing money issues and the cfo has only approved bryan for 2 jr developer positions at most. He has put out emails to his contacts within the industry asking for recommendations and is getting back a lot of new graduates. He doesn't mind going through the applicants if it means finding someone who will be a good fit for the job, but he vastly prefers experience to education which is limiting his pool of applicants.

Goals

Bryan is looking for developers with experience and practice, but haven't been tested in the industry yet. He is looking to hire brand-new graduates and immediately put them into the development team. He would like to see a body of work in front of the applicants and is not interested in someone who hasn't done some work on website design.

As an investigating user, I am looking for a jr developer who has a self-starter attitude, and a body of work to back it up to add to my team.
